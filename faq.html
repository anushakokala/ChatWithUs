FAQ

What are the best clubs to join as an engineering major?
There are plenty of clubs on campus for engineering majors! Some prominent clubs include American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME), Artificial Intelligence Society (AIS), Association of Computing Machinery (ACM), Biomedical Engineering Society (BMES), Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE), Women Who Compute (WWC), and Society of Asian Scientists and Engineers (SASE). Definitely check these clubs out!
What is the best way to get more experience?
Participating in hackathons, getting involved in clubs, and working on side projects are the best way to get more experience! Furthermore, these activities can be put on a resume to help you land an internship, which can help you work with real-world problems in your career field.
Is networking worth it in college?
Absolutely! Networking helps you get your name out there for potential recruiters and builds your reputation. Additionally, networking enables you to gain mentors and other peers who can help you gain more opportunities.

Is it better to focus more on your academics or your extracurriculars as a freshman?
Both are equally important! Having a strong GPA throughout college can ensure a timely graduation. However, while a good GPA is impressive, try to also participate in extracurriculars. Extracurriculars teach important skills you may not learn in a traditional classroom setting.
